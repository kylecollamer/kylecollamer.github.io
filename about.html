About Me

Hello, my name is Kyle Collamer. I am an aspiring software developer with a strong interest in building purposeful and user friendly digital solutions. My journey has been shaped equally by a passion for technology and a lifelong commitment to leadership, service, and personal growth through Scouting.

I began my Scouting journey in 2008 as a Cub Scout in Pack 213 in Murrysville, Pennsylvania. In 2013, I joined Troop 205, where I advanced through the program and embraced a variety of leadership opportunities. I completed National Youth Leadership Training in 2014, which served as the foundation for many of my future roles.

In 2015, I was inducted into the Order of the Arrow, becoming a member of Wagion Lodge Number 6. I became a Brotherhood Member in 2016 and was honored with the Vigil Honor in 2019. Since 2016, I have been a member of the Lodge Executive Committee, and I continue to serve in that capacity.

I served as Activities Chairman during parts of 2016, 2017, and 2018, and was Cub Scout Activity Chairman in 2018 and 2019. In 2019, I was elected Administrative Vice Chief and served during 2020 and 2021. I currently serve as the Unit Elections Advisor, following my time as the Unit Elections Chairman beginning in 2022.

In 2017, I joined the National Youth Leadership Training Staff as a Troop Guide. I returned to staff in 2023 as an Adult Leader, and became the Scoutmaster in 2024, a role I continue to hold. I became an Assistant Scoutmaster in 2020 and remain active with Troop 205. In 2021, I completed Wood Badge, the highest level of adult training in Scouting.

I also serve the Diocese of Greensburg as a Catholic Religious Emblems Counselor, guiding Scouts in earning the Ad Altare Dei and Pope Pius XII awards. I am a parishioner of Mount Saint Peter Parish in New Kensington, where faith and service are central parts of my life.

One of the most meaningful parts of my current involvement in Scouting is being an older brother to a Life Scout, and sharing in his experiences in the Order of the Arrow and in troop leadership.

I was accepted to Penn State University in 2019 and began my academic journey in 2020 at the New Kensington campus. I am finishing my degree through Penn State World Campus in May 2025, majoring in Software Engineering with a minor in Information Science and Technology. I also enjoy tutoring fellow students at Penn State New Kensington as a way of giving back to the faculty and the university community.

My technical experience includes programming in Java, C++, C, C#, Assembly, and Python, as well as web development and artificial intelligence fundamentals. I am always eager to expand my knowledge and continue learning.

In my free time, I enjoy playing video games, especially sandbox games like Minecraft, where I create mods, plugins, and modpacks. I also enjoy following motorsports, especially Formula One and endurance racing.

Through more than fifteen years in Scouting, I have developed a leadership philosophy rooted in preparation, responsibility, and servant leadership. These values influence every part of my life, from mentoring others to building software that solves real problems.
